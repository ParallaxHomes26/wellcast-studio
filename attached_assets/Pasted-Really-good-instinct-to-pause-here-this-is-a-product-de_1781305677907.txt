Really good instinct to pause here — this is a product design decision that affects how useful the tool feels every single day. Let me give you my honest take.

The problem with the card grid:

26 cards on one screen is overwhelming. Users will scroll, feel paralyzed by the volume, and not know where to start. The cards also treat a show notes draft the same as a hashtag string — very different assets that deserve very different amounts of attention.
The better approach — tabbed category navigation:
Think of how Notion or Linear handle dense information. A left sidebar or top tab bar with the six categories. Click a category, see only those assets. Clean, focused, one category at a time.
The user flow becomes:

Generation completes → land on Publishing & SEO tab automatically
Work through that category — copy show notes, copy SEO title, copy blog skeleton
Click Email tab → copy newsletter and subject lines
Click Social tab → copy captions, carousel, hooks
And so on through all six categories

This mirrors how they actually work — they don't do everything at once, they work platform by platform.

On downloading — yes, absolutely.
Two download options worth building:
Option A — Full PDF export

One beautifully formatted PDF with all 26 assets organized by category. Clean typography, your brand on it. They save it, share it with a VA, or print it. This is the highest-value download option.
Option B — Category-specific copy

A "Copy all" button within each category tab that copies everything in that section as formatted text. Fast, practical, works for people who just want to paste into Notion or a Google Doc.
Option C — Both

Full PDF download button in the header plus copy-all per category. This is what I'd recommend.
